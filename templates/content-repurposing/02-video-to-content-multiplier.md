# Video-to-Content Multiplier

## Description
Turn one video (YouTube, webinar, podcast, etc.) into 10+ pieces of written and social content. Extract key insights, create transcripts, clips, and quotable moments.

## Prompt Text
```
You are a content repurposing expert who turns one video into endless content.

I have a video about: [TOPIC]
Video type: [YOUTUBE / WEBINAR / PODCAST / INTERVIEW / LIVE STREAM]
Video length: [LENGTH - e.g., "45 minutes", "15 minutes"]
Key topics covered: [OUTLINE - e.g., "1. Why most people fail, 2. The framework, 3. Real example"]
Main insight: [PRIMARY TAKEAWAY]
Target audience: [WHO THIS HELPS - e.g., "early-stage founders", "marketing managers"]

Generate content from the video by:
1. Extracting the transcript (summarized if long)
2. Identifying 3-5 core ideas/frameworks
3. Creating quotable moments
4. Generating clips/excerpts perfect for social
5. Writing blog post from video
6. Creating email digest
7. Generating social posts with video clips
8. Creating podcast show notes if applicable

Output format:

**Video Overview:**
- Topic, length, main takeaway
- Key timestamps for important sections

**Transcript Summary:**
- 500-word summary of main ideas

**Quotable Moments (5-7):**
- Direct quotes perfect for graphics or posts
- Context for each quote

**Blog Post Idea:**
- Title, structure, key sections (ready to expand)

**Social Clips to Extract:**
- Timings for 3-4 key moments to turn into short videos/clips
- Why each is shareworthy

**Social Posts:**
- 4-5 posts across platforms (LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram)

**Email Summary:**
- 2-3 email variations highlighting key insights

Avoid:
- Transcribing the entire video word-for-word
- Over-extracting (pick the most valuable moments)
- Using quotes out of context
- Making clips without clear hooks

Format as a complete repurposing kit.
```

### TRANSCRIPT SUMMARY (500 words)

Most founders build first, ask customers later.

That's why 90% of startups fail.

Here's what works instead: Validate your idea in 2 weeks before writing a line of code.

**The Problem with Building First**

When you fall in love with an idea, you want to build immediately. It feels like progress. But progress toward *what*?

Most founders spend 6 months building a product nobody wants. Then they spend another 3 months trying to sell it. When the sales don't come, they blame marketing or the market.

Actually? They built the wrong thing.

**The 3-Step Validation Framework**

I've helped 20+ founders validate ideas. The ones who succeed follow a simple pattern:

**Step 1: Talk to 10 people (week 1)**
Don't ask "Would you use this?" People say yes to everything.
Instead, ask: "What's the biggest pain you have in [domain]?"
Listen for patterns. If 5+ people mention the same problem, you've found market need.

**Step 2: Pre-sell (week 2)**
Can you actually sell this idea before it exists?
Offer a discount for early access. "I'm building X. Would you pay $50/month for it?"
If people won't commit even with a 50% discount, you don't have a market.

**Step 3: Interview the "yes" people (week 2)**
Talk to the 2-3 people who said yes.
Ask what they'd want, what they'd pay, what problems they'd want solved first.
These conversations give you the roadmap.

**Real Founder Stories**

Founder A spent 4 months building a project management tool. Launched to silence. Validated *after* building.

Founder B spent 2 weeks talking to 15 product managers. Found 3 would pay $500/month for a specific feature. Validated *before* building. Now doing $50K/month.

Same market. Different approaches.

**The Biggest Mistakes**

Mistake 1: Asking friends if they'd use it (they'll say yes to be nice)
Mistake 2: Building multiple features (start with one)
Mistake 3: Overthinking the validation (2 weeks is enough)

**What Most Founders Miss**

The best part of validation? It's not saying "yes" or "no" to an idea.

It's understanding *why* people do or don't want it.

You'll learn more in 2 weeks of customer conversations than in 6 months of solo development.

**How to Start Monday**

1. Pick one problem you want to solve
2. Find 10 people who have that problem
3. Ask what their current solution is
4. Ask what doesn't work about it
5. Notice patterns

That's it. One week. Zero code. Clear direction.

## Pro Tips

**Tip 1: Extract Clips With Sound On**  
Video clips work best when they're extracted with natural sound (speaker's voice), not as silent B-roll. This keeps people watching on muted platforms like Instagram or Facebook.

**Tip 2: Use Timestamps in Your Video**  
If you're creating new video content, add timecode overlays (0:00, 5:00, 10:00) so it's easy to find the best moments later for repurposing.

**Tip 3: Create a "Quotables" Graphic Pack**  
For each quotable moment, create 3 versions (different background colors/styles). Schedule them over 3 weeks. Quotes get reposted by followers, extending your reach.
