# Instagram Caption Writer

## Description
Write Instagram captions that convert followers into customers, spark comments, and tell a story in 100-300 words. Includes carousel caption strategies and hashtag optimization.

## Prompt Text
```
You are an Instagram content strategist who writes captions that convert engagement into action.

I'm posting: [CONTENT TYPE - e.g., "a carousel teaching my design process", "a before/after of my office setup"]
My account focus: [NICHE - e.g., "design/productivity for creative entrepreneurs"]
Goal: [GOAL - e.g., "drive people to my free PDF guide", "spark debate about design tools"]
Visual description: [WHAT'S IN THE POST - e.g., "5 slides showing my workflow from sketch to final design"]
Target audience: [WHO YOU'RE TALKING TO - e.g., "freelance designers ages 25-40"]
Tone: [TONE - e.g., "personal and conversational", "educational and encouraging"]

Write an Instagram caption that:
1. Opens with a hook (problem, question, or curiosity) that stops the scroll
2. Tells a story or gives context (1-2 sentences; don't overexplain)
3. Delivers value (teaching, insight, or inspiration—not just showing off)
4. Ends with a genuine call-to-action (link in bio, a question, or an invitation)
5. Uses line breaks and white space (easier to read on mobile)
6. Includes 1-2 "transition words" that make it feel conversational ("Here's the thing," "Plot twist:")
7. If carousel: Briefly preview what people will see when they swipe

Avoid:
- Overusing emojis (max 3-4)
- Asking for likes/comments directly ("Like if you agree!")
- Generic motivation ("You've got this!")
- Long walls of text (use line breaks!)
- Hashtags in the caption (add them as a first comment instead)

Format as caption-ready text.
```

## Example Input
**Content Type:** Carousel with 8 slides showing my design critique framework  
**Account Focus:** Design/process education for junior designers  
**Goal:** Build authority and get people to follow for more design tips  
**Visual Description:** Slides showing common design mistakes and how to spot them in your own work  
**Target Audience:** Junior designers and design students, ages 20-30  
**Tone:** Encouraging but honest, like a mentor

## Example Output
```
Swipe through this one slowly.

Here's the thing: Most design critique happens *wrong*. People say "I don't like it" or "Make it pop" without explaining *why*.

That's why I created this framework.

These slides show you exactly what to look for when reviewing design work—whether it's your own portfolio, a client project, or feedback from your team.

What to spot:
1. Is the hierarchy clear? (Can you follow the visual flow?)
2. Do the colors work *together*? (Or do they fight?)
3. Is there white space, or does it feel cramped?
4. Does the type support the message? (Or undermine it?)
5. Is this solving the actual problem? (Or just looking pretty?)

Here's what shifted my design: I started asking *why* instead of saying what I didn't like.

Instead of "This doesn't feel right," I ask "Does the hierarchy guide your eye to the CTA?" Instead of "Make it pop," I ask "Is there enough contrast to read this on mobile?"

Suddenly, feedback became actionable. And my design got better faster.

Save this. Use it on your next project. See how much faster you improve when your critique has *direction*.

Drop a comment: What's one design mistake you find yourself repeating? (Mine was always poor white space management 👇)
```

## Pro Tips

**Tip 1: The First Line Is Your Hook—Keep It Short**  
Instagram shows the first 2 lines before "...more." Your opening needs to stop the scroll. Avoid introductions like "Hi everyone!" or "I'm so excited to share..." Start with the *value* or *curiosity*.

**Tip 2: Add Hashtags as a First Comment, Not in the Caption**  
Instagram punishes captions that look like hashtag spam. Write your caption clean, then comment with 10-15 relevant hashtags immediately after posting (before anyone else comments). Same reach, cleaner look.

**Tip 3: Use a Clear CTA but Make It Natural**  
"Drop a comment," "Save this," and "Link in bio" are the three CTAs that actually work on Instagram. Choose one and use it naturally. "Drop a comment: What's your biggest design struggle?" works better than "Comment below!"
