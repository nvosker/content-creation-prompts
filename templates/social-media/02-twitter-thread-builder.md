# Twitter/X Thread Builder

## Description
Structure and write viral Twitter threads that teach something valuable in 8-12 tweets. Includes hook formulas and pacing strategies to keep people reading until the last tweet.

## Prompt Text
```
You are a Twitter thread expert who writes threads that get thousands of likes and boost follower growth.

I want to share: [TOPIC/LESSON - e.g., "how to write better cold emails"]
Main concept/insight: [CORE IDEA - e.g., "most cold emails fail because they lead with a product pitch, not empathy"]
Thread length: [NUMBER OF TWEETS - e.g., 10 tweets]
Audience: [WHO YOU'RE TALKING TO - e.g., "early-stage founders and sales people"]
Tone: [TONE - e.g., "practical and a bit sarcastic"]

Write a Twitter thread that:
1. Opens with a hook (question, surprising stat, or bold claim) in Tweet 1
2. Acknowledges why people *currently* get it wrong (tweets 2-3)
3. Introduces your framework or insight (tweet 4)
4. Walks through 3-5 steps/ideas, one per tweet (tweets 5-8)
5. Wraps with a summary and call-to-action (last tweet)
6. Uses white space strategically (short sentences, line breaks)
7. Each tweet stands alone but connects to the larger story
8. Avoids jargon; explains like you're talking to a smart friend

Avoid:
- "Thread:" at the start (unnecessary)
- Emojis every tweet (looks desperate)
- Cliff-hangers that feel artificial
- "RT if you agree" or engagement bait
- Multiple threads in one (confusing)

Format as numbered tweets ready to copy/paste. Each tweet separated by [---].
```

## Example Input
**Topic:** How to find business ideas that actually have a market  
**Core Idea:** Most people start with an idea they love, then search for customers. Backwards.  
**Thread Length:** 10 tweets  
**Audience:** Aspiring entrepreneurs and indie hackers  
**Tone:** Direct, practical, slightly skeptical

## Pro Tips

**Tip 1: Make Each Tweet Valuable Standalone**  
Someone should be able to read just tweet 5 without following tweets 1-4 and still think "that's useful." This improves retweets and reach.

**Tip 2: Use Line Breaks Inside Tweets**  
Instead of one long paragraph, break tweets into 2-3 ideas with white space. It looks less dense and reads faster on mobile.

**Tip 3: Save the Best Insight for Tweet 8-9**  
Don't front-load all value in tweets 1-3. People stop scrolling if they feel they've gotten the main point. Bury one gold nugget in the latter half to keep them reading.
