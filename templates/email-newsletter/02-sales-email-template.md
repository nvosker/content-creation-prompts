# Sales Email Generator

## Description
Write high-converting sales emails that feel personal, not corporate. Includes frameworks for cold outreach, follow-ups, and closing emails that actually get responses.

## Prompt Text
```
You are an expert sales copywriter who writes emails that get responses, not deletion.

I'm emailing: [RECIPIENT DESCRIPTION - e.g., "marketing managers at e-commerce companies"]
Goal: [EMAIL GOAL - e.g., "get them on a discovery call"]
What I'm offering: [BRIEFLY - e.g., "email marketing strategy consultation"]
Length: [SHORT (50-75 words) / MEDIUM (75-150 words) / COLD OUTREACH / FOLLOW-UP]
Key benefit: [WHAT THEY GAIN - e.g., "recover abandoned cart revenue without new tools"]
Angle: [APPROACH - e.g., "specific problem they have", "result others got", "bold contrarian claim"]

Write a sales email that:
1. Leads with *their* problem, not your solution
2. Shows you understand their world specifically (not generic)
3. References something recent/relevant (if possible)
4. Reveals the result or benefit briefly
5. Has one clear call-to-action (usually a calendar link or question)
6. Sounds like a real person, not a template
7. Never asks for the sale directly—ask for a conversation

Email structure:
- Subject line: [Brief, curiosity-driven or relevance-driven]
- Opening: [Relevance hook—show you know them]
- Problem: [What they're struggling with]
- Solution: [Briefly—what you do]
- Proof/result: [How others benefited, or a stat]
- CTA: [Ask for a call or conversation]
- Sign-off: [Casual, personal]

Avoid:
- "Thought of you" or other fake personalization
- Overselling ("You're going to love this!")
- Too many CTAs (pick one)
- Long case studies in the email (save for call)
- Desperation ("I'd love to..." or "Would you be open to...")

Format as a complete email ready to send.
```

## Example Input
**Recipient:** Marketing manager at a mid-market SaaS company  
**Goal:** Get them on a 20-minute discovery call  
**Offering:** Email marketing optimization (specifically for abandoned trial conversions)  
**Length:** Medium (100-150 words)  
**Key Benefit:** Recover $50K+ in annual revenue from trial users without paid ads  
**Angle:** They're probably leaving trial revenue on the table because most trial users never see a structured nurture sequence

## Example Output
```
Subject: Your trial users aren't seeing the real value (yet)

---

Hi [First Name],

I was looking at [Company Name]'s trial flow—sent new users straight to the product with no onboarding sequence.

Which is interesting because most SaaS companies leave 35-40% of trial revenue on the table for exactly this reason.

The ones recovering it? They run a simple 4-email sequence during the trial: 
- Day 1: Here's what you can do in 5 minutes (video-focused)
- Day 3: Here's how similar companies use this
- Day 6: You're halfway through—here's what you might be missing
- Day 9: Your trial ends in 1 day (soft sell, not pushy)

Average result: 18% → 28% trial-to-paid conversion. For a company processing 500 trials/month, that's an extra $50K+/year in annual revenue.

Zero new tools. Just better sequencing.

Curious if this is something your team has tried, or if the trial experience has been pretty much the same?

If you want to chat about how other companies in your space are handling this, I've got 20 minutes Friday afternoon.

[Calendar link]

[Your name]
```

## Pro Tips

**Tip 1: Reference Something Specific to Prove You Did Research**  
"I noticed you're a SaaS company" is generic. "I noticed your trial flow sends people straight to the product without onboarding" shows you actually looked. Specific beats flattery every time.

**Tip 2: Lead With Their Problem, End With Your Solution**  
Most sales emails do it backwards: "We help SaaS companies increase trial conversions..." Start with their world instead: "Your trial users probably aren't seeing the full value in the first few days..." This is less salesy and more relatable.

**Tip 3: Make Your CTA Specific and Friction-Free**  
Instead of "Would you be open to a call?" try "I've got 20 minutes Friday afternoon if you want to dig deeper." Include a calendar link. Reduce friction = more responses.
