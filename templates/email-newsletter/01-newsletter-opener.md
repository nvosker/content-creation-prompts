# Newsletter Opener Generator

## Description
Write compelling newsletter openings (intro sections) that make readers want to keep reading. Includes personal storytelling, hooks, and transitions to the main content.

## Prompt Text
```
You are an expert email writer who hooks readers in the first 50 words.

I'm writing a newsletter issue about: [TOPIC/THEME]
My newsletter's tone: [TONE - e.g., "personal and conversational", "educational and authoritative"]
Key theme of this issue: [MAIN IDEA - e.g., "why most people fail at cold outreach"]
Opening hook: [WHAT YOU WANT TO LEAD WITH - e.g., "a personal story", "a surprising statistic", "a contrarian take"]
Audience: [WHO YOU'RE WRITING TO - e.g., "solopreneurs and consultants"]

Write a newsletter opening (50-150 words) that:
1. Grabs attention in the first 1-2 sentences (no "Hey there!" or generic pleasantries)
2. Reveals the reason you're writing about this topic now
3. Mentions the problem your readers face (without solving it yet)
4. Teases the value they'll get in this issue
5. Transitions smoothly into the main content

Structure:
- Hook (1-2 sentences) — surprise, story, or insight
- Context (2-3 sentences) — why now? Why this matters?
- Transition (1 sentence) — "Here's what you'll learn..."

Avoid:
- "In this week's issue..." (boring)
- Emojis (unprofessional in newsletters)
- Overpromising ("Life-changing insights!")
- Asking them to do anything yet

Format as email-ready text.
```

## Example Input
**Topic:** Why your personal brand isn't working (and what actually does)  
**Newsletter Tone:** Direct but friendly, slightly irreverent  
**Key Theme:** Most people think personal branding = Instagram fitness photos, but it's actually about demonstrating competence  
**Hook:** Personal story (I tried everything and failed until I changed my approach)  
**Audience:** Freelancers and consultants trying to build authority

## Example Output
```
I spent 18 months trying to "build my personal brand."

I wrote daily LinkedIn posts. I optimized my Instagram. I recorded 50 videos. And nothing happened. My audience barely grew. People didn't reach out. I got maybe one inbound opportunity from 12 months of posting.

The worst part? I was watching *other* people with 1/10th the content output get 10x the results.

Then I realized: I was doing personal branding completely wrong.

I was thinking of it like marketing—make content, get attention, build a following. But that's not how personal branding works. Real personal branding is about becoming known for solving a specific problem better than anyone else. It's not about being famous. It's about being credible.

Once I shifted from "create more content" to "demonstrate clear expertise on one thing," everything changed.

This week, I'm breaking down what actually works, where people go wrong, and the exact framework that got me results in 3 months instead of 18.

Let's dig in:
```

## Pro Tips

**Tip 1: Lead With Vulnerability or Surprise**  
Start with "I was wrong about..." or a surprising stat. Don't start with "Let's talk about..." Vulnerability or surprise stops people from deleting the email.

**Tip 2: Show Why NOW**  
Mention something timely: "I noticed X happening in my community..." or "A conversation last week made me think..." This shows the newsletter isn't pre-written; it's for *them*, *now*.

**Tip 3: Keep It Under 150 Words**  
Readers scan intros in 10 seconds. If it's long, they'll jump to the main content. Short, punchy intros work better. Save the long-form storytelling for the main content.
