# Re-engagement Campaign Generator

## Description
Build a series of emails designed to win back inactive subscribers before removing them from your list. Includes psychological hooks and last-chance strategies.

## Prompt Text
```
You are an email strategist who wins back disengaged subscribers through clever, non-pushy re-engagement campaigns.

My email list issue: [DESCRIBE - e.g., "500 subscribers haven't opened an email in 6+ months"]
My niche/business: [NICHE - e.g., "productivity coaching"]
What I offer: [BRIEFLY - e.g., "weekly productivity tips and templates"]
Why they might have disengaged: [REASON - e.g., "I changed my email style", "they signed up but never engaged much"]
Campaign length: [2 / 3 / 4 EMAIL SEQUENCE]
Goal: [GOAL - e.g., "get 20% of them to re-engage, remove the rest"]

Write a re-engagement campaign (email series) that:
1. Acknowledges the gap without guilt-tripping
2. Reminds them why they subscribed in the first place
3. Shares what's changed/improved since they last engaged
4. Gives them a choice (stay subscribed and get more value, or unsubscribe—no hard feelings)
5. Uses psychological triggers like FOMO, relevance, and curiosity
6. Makes it easy to opt out if they're not interested (reduces spam complaints)

Email structure for re-engagement:
Email 1: "We've missed you" + What's new + Light ask (open email 2)
Email 2: Value dump (share the best stuff from recent emails) + Curiosity hook
Email 3: [Optional] "Last chance" psychology + Final reminder + Easy unsubscribe option
Email 4: [Optional] Win-back offer (discount, bonus, exclusive content)

Key principle: Make it about *them* reconnecting, not about *you* recovering deliverability.

Avoid:
- Guilt-tripping ("We haven't heard from you!")
- Overselling ("You're missing out!")
- Making unsubscribe hard (this kills trust)
- Fake personalization ("I noticed you...")
- Multiple CTAs (keep focus)

Format as complete, copy-ready emails.
```

## Example Input
**List Issue:** 600 inactive subscribers (no opens in 4+ months)  
**Niche:** Email marketing education  
**What I Offer:** Weekly email tips, templates, and case studies  
**Reason for Disengagement:** Probably my emails became too sales-focused and lost the teaching value  
**Campaign Length:** 3 emails  
**Goal:** Rebuild trust, recover 25% of inactive subscribers, clean up the list

```
CAMPAIGN NOTES:
- Email 1 is honest and self-aware (humans respond to honesty better than salesy apologies)
- Email 2 delivers pure value with no ask (builds goodwill)
- Email 3 is the "last chance" email with easy unsubscribe (respects their choice, reduces spam complaints)
- Spacing: Days 0, 2, 5 (enough time between emails, but quick enough to re-engage before they forget about you)
- Psychology: Combination of curiosity (what's new?), relevance (showing you've changed), and choice architecture (easy to re-engage or leave)
```

## Pro Tips

**Tip 1: Make Unsubscribe Easy**  
Offer a clear unsubscribe button in your re-engagement email. This feels risky, but it reduces spam complaints and builds trust with re-engaged subscribers. The people who unsubscribe probably wouldn't have engaged anyway.

**Tip 2: Lead With What Changed, Not What You Want**  
Don't say "We miss you and want you back!" Instead say "Here's what's different now." This shifts the conversation from you needing them to them missing what you offer.

**Tip 3: Show, Don't Tell**  
Email 2 should be a value dump—actual frameworks, templates, insights. Don't just say "we have great content now." Show it. Prove it. Make them want more.
