# Welcome Sequence Generator

## Description
Build a complete welcome email sequence (3-5 emails) that turns new subscribers into engaged readers and customers. Includes segmentation strategies and conversion optimization. Design automated email flows that nurture leads and drive consistent, predictable revenue.

## Prompt Text
```
You are an email strategist who builds welcome sequences that convert cold subscribers into warm, engaged customers.

My product/service: [BRIEF - e.g., "email marketing courses"]
My audience: [WHO SUBSCRIBES - e.g., "beginner content creators"]
Welcome sequence length: [3 / 4 / 5 EMAILS]
Main goal: [GOAL - e.g., "get them to buy the course", "warm them up for a sales call"]
Key value you provide: [e.g., "teach email marketing without jargon"]

For each email in the sequence, write:
1. **Subject line** (compelling but not clickbait)
2. **Email body** (150-300 words, conversational tone)
3. **Main purpose** (what this email does for the sequence)
4. **CTA** (what you want them to do)

Sequence strategy:
Email 1 (Delivered immediately): Welcome + deliver promised freebie + get to know them
Email 2 (Day 2): Tell a story that shows your authority / share a key insight
Email 3 (Day 4): Deeper value—teach something or share social proof
Email 4 (Day 7): [Optional] Introduce your paid product or offer a call
Email 5 (Day 10): [Optional] Final value or last chance on offer

Rules for welcome sequences:
- Email 1 should deliver the promised opt-in freebie
- Don't sell in email 1 (build trust first)
- Each email should stand alone but connect to the story
- Use white space and short paragraphs (mobile-first)
- Include segmentation question in email 1 (so you can personalize later)
- Gradually warm them up to a paid offer (don't dump sales pitch)

Format as complete, copy-ready emails with subject lines.
```

## Advanced Customization Section

### Variation 1: Short Sequence (2-3 emails)
For simple products or good fit audiences. Email 1: Freebie + engagement. Email 2: Value. Email 3: Offer. This works for time-sensitive offers or when audience is already warm.

### Variation 2: Long Sequence (6-7 emails)
For high-ticket offers or colder audiences. Spend more time building trust. More stories, more social proof, more objection handling. Best for $500+ products/services.

### Variation 3: Segmented Sequence (Different paths based on Email 1 answer)
Email 1 asks: "Are you building a list or growing an existing one?" Path A goes to growth-focused emails. Path B goes to beginning-focused emails. This personalization increases conversion 20-30%.

### Variation 4: Product-Led Sequence
Instead of educational emails, let the product do the work. Email 1: Freebie walkthrough. Email 2: Feature benefit. Email 3: Success story of product usage. Email 4: Upgrade offer. Works well for SaaS and software products.

### Variation 5: Relationship-First Sequence
Focus on building personal connection before pitching anything. Email 1: Story about why you started this. Email 2: Personal vulnerability (lesson learned). Email 3: Your philosophy on this problem. Email 4: Only now, soft introduction to offer. Best for premium/luxury positioning.

### Variation 6: Fast-Action Sequence
Create scarcity/urgency. Email 1: Freebie. Email 2: Limited offer (available 48 hours). Email 3: Reminder, offer ends tomorrow. Email 4: Last chance email. Works for time-bound events, launches, or limited-seat offers.

## Advanced Pro Tips (Revenue Optimization)

**Tip 1: Segment by Engagement Level**  
People who open all emails are high-intent. People who open 1-2 are low-intent. After email 3, run different paths: High-intent gets immediate offer. Low-intent gets 2 more value emails before offering. This increases conversion by 30-40%.

**Tip 2: Use Scarcity Strategically (Only When Genuine)**  
"This offer expires Friday" works if it's true. Don't overuse scarcity or people stop believing you. Use it 1-2 times per sequence, and only for legitimate time-bound offers (launch, seasonal, limited seats).

**Tip 3: Test Subject Lines Ruthlessly**  
Subject line is everything. Test: Question vs. Statement. Urgency vs. curiosity. Numbers vs. no numbers. Send sequence to 500 people and compare. Then scale what wins. A/B testing subject lines can improve open rate by 20-40%.

**Tip 4: Include a Segmentation Question in Email 1**  
Ask: "What's your biggest challenge?" or "Are you here to [A] or [B]?" This lets you tag people and potentially run different future sequences. People who see emails about their specific challenge convert 2-3x higher than generic broadcasts.

**Tip 5: Create Follow-Up Sequences for Non-Openers**  
30% of people don't open the initial sequence. Send them a separate sequence: "Noticed you missed this email..." or "Here's what you missed..." High urgency + curiosity often makes them engage with the offer when others have already moved on.
