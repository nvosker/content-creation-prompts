# Blog Outline Generator

## Description
Transform a topic or keyword into a comprehensive, SEO-friendly blog outline with clear section headers, talking points, and content depth guidance. Perfect for writers who want structure before drafting.

## Prompt Text
```
You are an expert blog strategist and content outline specialist. 

I'm writing a blog post on: [TOPIC/KEYWORD]
Target audience: [READER DESCRIPTION - e.g., "beginners in digital marketing"]
Word count goal: [TARGET WORDS - e.g., "2,000 words"]
Tone: [TONE - e.g., "professional but approachable"]

Create a detailed blog outline with:
1. Compelling headline (and 2 alternative headlines)
2. Hook/introduction strategy (1-2 sentences explaining what I'll cover)
3. Clear main sections with:
   - Section title
   - 3-5 key talking points per section
   - Suggested content depth (e.g., "explain with 1 example")
   - Recommended paragraph count
4. Conclusion strategy (summary + call-to-action)
5. SEO metadata: suggested meta description (under 160 chars) and 5 long-tail keyword variations

Format as a clean, actionable outline I can follow while drafting.
```

## Example Input
**Topic:** How to Start a Freelance Writing Career  
**Target Audience:** Aspiring writers with 0-2 years experience  
**Word Count:** 2,500 words  
**Tone:** Motivational but practical

## Example Output
```
## Headline
Main: "The Complete Guide to Starting a Freelance Writing Career (2024)"
Alt 1: "How to Build Your First Freelance Writing Business from Zero"
Alt 2: "Freelance Writing for Beginners: Your 6-Month Action Plan"

## Hook Strategy
Open with: A relatable struggle (blank page, imposter syndrome) + promise 
(concrete steps to land first clients in 90 days)

## Main Sections

### 1. Why Freelance Writing is Perfect Now (300-400 words)
- Growing demand for quality content across industries
- Flexibility and work-from-anywhere appeal
- Lower barriers to entry vs. traditional publishing
- Real income potential (rates overview: $0.10/word → $1+/word)
- Include: 1 success story or statistic

### 2. Assess Your Writing Niche (400-500 words)
- Why niche matters (easier to price, build authority, attract clients)
- Exercises: What you know, what you love, what pays
- Common niches: B2B/SaaS, health/wellness, finance, lifestyle, tech
- How to test niches without years of experience
- Include: Personal niche-selection framework

### 3. Build Your Writer Platform (500-600 words)
- Portfolio: Samples, case studies, or ghost-written work
- Website essentials (cheap, simple options)
- LinkedIn optimization (copywriting tips)
- Medium/Substack as free portfolio builders
- Include: Checklist of 5 must-haves for credibility

### 4. Landing Your First Clients (400-500 words)
- Where to find freelance gigs (Upwork, Contently, direct outreach)
- Pitch templates and what works
- Pricing as a beginner (hourly vs. per-project)
- Initial rate strategy (undercut or fair market?)
- Include: Sample pitch template

### 5. Growing Your Rate & Reputation (300-400 words)
- How to raise rates without losing clients
- Retainer vs. project work
- Building long-term client relationships
- The financial trajectory (first 12 months realistic numbers)
- Include: Common mistake to avoid

## Conclusion
- Recap the 5-step roadmap
- CTA: "Start with your niche assessment today" + link to template

## SEO Metadata
**Meta Description:** "Learn how to start a freelance writing career with zero experience. Step-by-step guide covering niches, portfolios, finding clients, and pricing."

**Long-tail Keywords:**
1. "how to start freelance writing career for beginners"
2. "freelance writing jobs no experience needed"
3. "how to find freelance writing clients"
4. "freelance writing rates 2024"
5. "best freelance writing platforms beginners"
```

## Pro Tips

**Tip 1: Adjust Section Word Count to Pacing**  
The word counts above are suggestions. If you're writing conversational, go 20% higher per section; if punchy and listicle-style, reduce by 20%. Always allocate ~10% of total words to intro and outro.

**Tip 2: Test Section Order with Skimmers**  
Before drafting, reorder sections by what a **skimmer** would want first (usually "why" → "how"). Use H2 for main sections so they appear in the table of contents—improves SEO and user experience.

**Tip 3: Embed Evidence Early**  
Strongest stat or quote should land in your hook or first section. Beginners often bury the best proof in section 3. Move it up to build credibility fast.
