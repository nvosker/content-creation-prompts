# Voiceover Script Generator

## Description
Write professional voiceover scripts for explainer videos, product demos, educational content, and commercials. Includes pacing, emphasis, and visual sync notes.

## Prompt Text
```
You are a voiceover scriptwriter who writes clear, engaging narration for video.

I'm creating a voiceover for: [PROJECT TYPE - e.g., "product demo video", "explainer animation", "course lesson", "commercial"]
Project topic: [TOPIC - e.g., "how to use the new email template feature"]
Target audience: [WHO WATCHES - e.g., "small business owners", "SaaS users", "students"]
Video type: [EXPLAINER / DEMO / EDUCATIONAL / COMMERCIAL / PROMOTIONAL]
Video length: [LENGTH - e.g., "90 seconds", "5 minutes"]
Tone: [TONE - e.g., "friendly and conversational", "professional and authoritative", "energetic and motivating"]
Key message: [MAIN POINT - e.g., "this feature saves 5 hours per week"]
Style: [STYLE - e.g., "B-roll voiceover", "talking head", "animation narration"]

Write a voiceover script that:

1. **Opens with a hook** (attention-grabbing opening line)
2. **Clearly states the problem or benefit** (what's the value?)
3. **Walks through the solution step-by-step** (how-to or explanation)
4. **Uses natural, conversational language** (like talking to a friend, not reading a script)
5. **Syncs with visuals** (notes on what's shown when you're speaking)
6. **Includes pacing notes** (where to pause, where to speed up, where to emphasize)
7. **Ends with a clear call-to-action** (what should they do?)
8. **Matches the tone naturally** (energetic, calm, professional, warm)

Format:

For each paragraph/section:
- [VISUAL: What's shown on screen]
- SPOKEN: (exact words to say, with delivery notes in parentheses)
- Timing: Rough seconds
- Emphasis notes: (what to emphasize, where to pause, tone cues)

Rules for voiceover:
- Write for the ear, not the eye (shorter sentences, conversational)
- Avoid jargon (or explain it if you must use it)
- Read it aloud before considering it done (does it sound natural?)
- Natural pauses matter (don't rush to fill silence)
- Match the energy to your audience and message
- Tech features → practical benefit ("saves 5 hours/week" not "API integration")

Avoid:
- Corporate speak ("we're excited to announce...")
- Overly formal tone unless truly warranted
- Reading directly from a script (sounds stiff)
- Unclear jargon without explanation
- Too many ideas in one sentence (break it up)
- Mismatched tone (energetic voiceover with slow visuals)

Make it sound natural, clear, and engaging. Something someone would actually enjoy listening to.
```

## Example Input
**Project Type:** Product demo (SaaS email platform)  
**Topic:** How to set up an automated email sequence  
**Target Audience:** Small business owners (non-technical)  
**Video Type:** Demo (screen recording + voiceover)  
**Length:** 2.5 minutes  
**Tone:** Friendly, reassuring, conversational  
**Key Message:** "You can automate customer follow-ups without technical skills"  
**Style:** B-roll of screen recording with voiceover

## DELIVERY GUIDE FOR VOICEOVER ARTIST

**Overall Tone:** Friendly, warm, conversational—like a knowledgeable friend teaching you something cool.

**Energy Level:** 6/10. Not peppy, not slow. Calm and reassuring. Match the pace of the visuals (if showing screen recording, don't rush ahead).

**Pacing:**
- Speak slower than normal conversation (people need time to process what they're seeing)
- Pause after key points (let them sink in)
- Don't fill every silence with talking—some quiet moments let the visuals breathe

**Emphasis:**
- Emphasize benefit phrases ("five minutes," "automatic," "while you sleep")
- Emphasize reassurance ("it's easy," "don't worry," "that's it")
- Don't over-emphasize (avoid sounding like a used car salesman)

**Pronunciation Clarity:**
- Enunciate clearly (viewers often watch without sound on)
- Slow down on technical terms if used
- But keep it conversational (don't sound like a robot)

**Word Emphasis Examples:**
- "You can try this FREE right now" (emphasize free)
- "It just... WORKS" (pause before, emphasize the payoff)
- "Five MINUTES to set up" (emphasize the speed)
- "Completely AUTOMATIC" (emphasize the benefit)

**Things to Avoid:**
- Don't sound corporate or robotic
- Don't talk too fast (let visuals catch up)
- Don't apologize ("Um," "uh," "like," "you know")
- Don't sound overly enthusiastic (it feels fake)
- Don't make it about features ("API integration") instead of benefits ("automatic")

## RECORDING NOTES

**Best Practices:**
- Record in a quiet room
- Use a good microphone (even a USB condenser microphone works)
- Record several takes of longer sections
- Take breaks between takes (voice gets tired)
- Listen back as you go (catch weird pronunciations early)

**Editing Notes:**
- Remove breaths between sentences (they sound unnatural)
- Add very slight fade-outs on pauses (not abrupt)
- Sync with visuals (record with timing notes, then align to video)
- Leave room for sound effects or music (don't make it too dense)

**File Format:**
- Export as WAV (best quality) or high-bitrate MP3
- 48kHz sample rate (standard for video)
- Mono or stereo (stereo if you have music playing underneath)

## Pro Tips

**Tip 1: Read It Aloud Multiple Times**  
If a sentence feels awkward reading it silently, it'll feel awkward when spoken. Read every script aloud multiple times. Let it become natural. Then record.

**Tip 2: Write for Pauses, Not Words**  
The best voiceovers have strategic pauses. Silence can be more powerful than words. Write your script expecting 1-2 second pauses after key points. It gives the visuals time to land and the listener time to absorb.

**Tip 3: Match Pace to Visuals**  
If you're showing a 30-second screen recording, don't speed-read a 20-second voiceover. Sync your pacing to what people are seeing. Slow down when showing complex steps. Speed up when transitioning between ideas.
