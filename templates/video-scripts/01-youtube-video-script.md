# YouTube Video Script Generator

## Description
Write complete YouTube video scripts with hooks, sections, B-roll cues, pacing, and CTAs. Includes SEO optimization and retention-focused structure.

## Prompt Text
```
You are a YouTube scriptwriter who writes videos that hook viewers, keep them watching, and make them take action.

I want to create a YouTube video about: [TOPIC]
Video length: [5 MIN / 10 MIN / 15 MIN / 20 MIN]
Video type: [TUTORIAL / STORY / OPINION / ANALYSIS / HOW-TO]
Target audience: [WHO WATCHES - e.g., "small business owners", "indie hackers"]
Main goal: [GOAL - e.g., "teach a framework", "build authority", "drive to product"]
Hook concept: [YOUR OPENING HOOK - e.g., "surprising stat", "bold claim", "curiosity gap"]

Write a complete script that includes:

1. **Hook (0-15 seconds)**
   - Opening line (must stop the scroll for YouTube home feed)
   - Optional context or story setup
   - Promise of what they'll learn

2. **Intro/Pattern Interrupt (15-45 seconds)**
   - Restate the promise
   - Preview the structure (what you'll cover)
   - Make them feel smart for clicking

3. **Main Content (varies by length)**
   - Organized in logical sections (every 2-3 min, change visuals)
   - Use specific examples and stories
   - Include "pattern interrupts" (unexpected moments, graphics, footage changes)
   - Avoid info dumps (break complex ideas into digestible chunks)

4. **Retention Cues**
   - Notes on when to show graphics, B-roll, or cut to different angle
   - "Person talking is boring" — switch visuals every 20-30 seconds
   - Pacing notes (when to slow down, when to quicken)

5. **Calls-to-Action**
   - Subscribe mention (natural, not pushy)
   - Comment prompt (specific question that invites response)
   - End screen cards (link to related videos, playlist, or external link)

6. **Closing (last 30 seconds)**
   - Recap of main insight
   - Clear CTA (subscribe, check description, watch next video)
   - Tease next video (curiosity hook for next click)

For each section, include:
- Exact spoken words (what you say on camera)
- [VISUAL CUE] (what's shown, B-roll, graphics, etc.)
- Timing notes
- Notes on tone/delivery (energetic, thoughtful, casual, etc.)

Format as a complete, production-ready script.

Avoid:
- "Hey guys!" or other outdated openers
- Repetitive content (don't teach the same thing twice in one video)
- Long monologues (break with visuals, graphics, B-roll)
- Weak hooks that don't make promises
- Fake enthusiasm or forced energy
- Unclear CTAs ("Let me know what you think!" is vague)

Make it conversational, specific, and engaging.
```

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Pacing:**
- Sections alternate between you talking (2-3 min) and visual content (graphics, B-roll)
- Never more than 30 seconds of you talking without visual change
- Slower sections (story examples) = 4-5 min, keeps engagement high

**Visuals/B-Roll Checklist:**
- Screen recordings: Demo validation form, customer notes
- Animated graphics: Steps, mistakes, comparisons
- Interview clips (if you have them): Real founder or customer
- You on camera: 50-60% of video
- Other visuals: 40-50%

**Music:**
- Upbeat but not obnoxious during intro/outro
- Calm/focus music during content sections
- Silence occasionally (gives weight to important points)

**Graphics/Text Overlays:**
- Title slide at beginning
- Section headers (when you introduce new step)
- Key stats or numbers (visualized)
- Call-outs for important points
- Subscribe button reminder (subtle, not pushy)

**SEO/YouTube Optimization:**
- Title: "Validate Your Business Idea in 2 Weeks (The Framework)"
- Description: Starts with 2-3 sentence summary, then all the links
- Tags: "business validation", "startup", "founder", "how to validate idea", "business idea validation"
- Thumbnail: You with surprised/confident expression + title text + color contrast

## Pro Tips

**Tip 1: Hook in the First 5 Seconds**  
YouTube viewers decide to stay or skip in the first 5 seconds. Your hook must make a promise or create curiosity. "Most founders build for 6 months then fail" is stronger than "Today I want to talk about validation."

**Tip 2: Change Visuals Every 30 Seconds Max**  
You talking to camera is boring. Shift between you, screen recordings, animations, B-roll. This keeps people watching and improves retention metrics.

**Tip 3: Make Your CTA Specific**  
Instead of "Subscribe if you like this," try "Subscribe and I'll send you the validation template in next week's video." Specific CTAs get higher engagement than generic ones.
